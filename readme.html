Superstore Sales Data Analysis

This documentation summarizes three key analyses performed using Python and related data science libraries. The analyses include:

  • Pareto Analysis of product sub-categories by profit.
  • Time Series Analysis of sales and profit over time.
  • Correlation Analysis between discount and profit.

1. Pareto Analysis

This analysis follows the 80/20 rule to identify which product sub-categories contribute the most to the overall profit. By sorting and calculating cumulative profit percentages, we visualize which few categories drive most of the profitability.

Conclusion: A small number of sub-categories contribute disproportionately to the overall profit, supporting the Pareto principle.

2. Time Series Analysis

We analyzed trends in sales and profit over time using line plots on monthly aggregated data. This helps understand seasonality and long-term business performance.

Conclusion: Sales tend to show seasonal patterns, and there are fluctuations in profitability that require further investigation for decision-making.

3. Discount vs Profit

Using scatter plots and linear regression, we examined the relationship between discounts offered and the resulting profit.

Conclusion: There is a weak to moderate negative correlation between discount and profit, suggesting that high discounts often reduce profitability.
